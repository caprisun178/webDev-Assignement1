Rome

Rome's history spans from a mythical founding in 753 BC as a village to a vast empire controlling Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, evolving through kingdoms, a powerful Republic, and autocratic Emperors, eventually becoming the center of Christianity and the Renaissance, and finally the capital of unified Italy in 1870, leaving lasting legacies in law, language, and governance. Its story involves legendary founders like Romulus and Remus, military expansion, incorporation of conquered peoples, periods of immense wealth and civil strife, and significant cultural growth.
